Adoption
Planning

Knowing where AI can help is not the same as making it happen. The space between “we’ve identified AI opportunities” and “AI is actually working inside our organisation” is filled with decisions that need to be resolved before anything gets built.

Engagement

Strategic & Advisory

Typical Duration

2 – 4 weeks

Focus & Stack

AI StrategyGovernanceChange ManagementROIRoadmapRisk Management

We turn identified AI opportunities into a practical rollout plan that accounts for real constraints: budget, team capability, infrastructure, regulatory environment, and appetite for change. Strategy without execution planning is just a wish list.

What the plan covers

Prioritisation & Sequencing

Which opportunities first, which to stage, which to set aside. Structured into three horizons: quick wins (weeks), foundation building (months), and strategic initiatives (longer-term).

Build vs. Buy Analysis

For each priority opportunity: custom solution, off-the-shelf product, or existing platform configuration? Total cost of ownership, not just licence fees.

Team & Capability Planning

What skills are needed, do they exist internally, should they be hired or engaged through a partner? Specific to prompt engineering, RAG, data engineering, ML ops.

Governance Framework

AI use policy, model inventory, review workflows, quality monitoring, incident response. Proportionate to risk, not bureaucratic gatekeeping.

Change Management

Champion programs, training sequences, feedback loops, communication plans, measurable adoption targets.

Risk Management

Accuracy, data, operational, regulatory, reputational, and vendor risk. Each with a mitigation strategy.

Deliverables

What you get

  • Phased adoption roadmap with timelines, milestones, and decision gates
  • Prioritised initiative list with build/buy recommendations
  • Governance framework ready for implementation
  • Change management plan
  • Business case with ROI projections suitable for board approval
